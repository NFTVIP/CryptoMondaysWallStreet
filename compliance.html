Do Your Own Research (DYOR)

Digital assets, cryptocurrencies, and blockchain-based investments are highly speculative and carry substantial risk of loss. Before making any investment or financial decision related to digital assets, you should:

  • Independently verify all information through multiple reliable sources
  • Consult with a licensed financial advisor, attorney, or tax professional
  • Understand that past performance is not indicative of future results
  • Assess your own risk tolerance and financial situation
  • Read all relevant documentation, whitepapers, and regulatory filings
  • Understand that you may lose all capital invested in digital assets

Digital Asset Risk Disclosures

Participation in digital asset markets involves significant risks, including but not limited to:

  • Volatility: Digital asset prices can fluctuate dramatically in short periods, including total loss of value
  • Regulatory Risk: The regulatory environment for digital assets is evolving and may change in ways that adversely affect the value or legality of digital assets
  • Liquidity Risk: Digital asset markets may lack liquidity, making it difficult to buy or sell assets at desired prices
  • Technology Risk: Digital assets rely on technology that may be subject to bugs, hacks, or failures
  • Custody Risk: Loss of private keys or access credentials may result in permanent loss of assets
  • Fraud Risk: The digital asset space contains a high prevalence of scams, fraud, and bad actors
